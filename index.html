About Me



Sina Mohseni

G. Scholar LinkedIn Github e-Mail

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. candidate at Texas A&M University. I am pursuing my PhD at INDIE Lab working with Dr. Eric Ragan. My main research interests include visual analytics and human-computer interaction. I also am interested in studying human and interpretable machine learning communications and designing visual analytics systems to ease this communication. My current research focus is on multiple topics from analytic provenance segmentation to HCI research on Explainable Machine Learning. My work on analytic provenance investigates the use of user interaction logs for finding the analysis segments for a meaningful summarized provenance visualization and meta-analysis. My work on interpretable machine learning includes evaluating machines local explanations with human subjects and designing user interfaces for interpretable machine learning to highlight explanation importance in rea-world cases.

I have always been seeking to solve deep problems in my research and have never been tired of working. I believe being innovative, goal-oriented and my skillful leadership helped me the most throughout my research. Prior to joining Texas A&M, I studied M.Sc. in Electronic Systems Engineering at Babol Noshirvany university of Technology, working on computer vision systems and optimization algorithms. I also earned a B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from University of Isfahan, Iran.
