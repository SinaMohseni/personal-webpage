I am a Computer Science Ph.D. candidate at Texas A&M University. I am pursuing my PhD at INDIE Lab working with Dr. Eric Ragan. My main research interest is interpretable machine learning and visual analytics. I current work on interpretable machine learning (or explainable AI) which goes under machine learning, human-computer interaction, and visual analytics fields. My research includes the design and evaluation of machines learning explanations in forms of instance and model explanations. I design visualizations and interfaces for end-users to understand and interact with machine explanations and evaluate the explanations usefulness for end-users. Before that, I was mainly working on analytic provenance visualization and segmentation which is about analyzing user interaction logs for finding the analysis segments for a meaningful summarized provenance for meta-analysis.

I have always been seeking to solve deep problems in my research and have never given up on my ideas. I believe being creative, goal-oriented and my hardworking helped me the most throughout my research. Prior to joining Texas A&M, I was studying at Oregon State University for one year. I studied M.Sc. in Electronic Systems Engineering at Babol Noshirvany university of Technology, working on computer vision (facial expression recognition) and meta-heuristic optimization algorithms. I also earned a B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from University of Isfahan, Iran.
